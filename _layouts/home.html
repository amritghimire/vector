About

VECTOR – Direct your Vision is a national level technical festival of Paschimanchal Campus organized by Free Students Union, Paschimanchal Campus in association with different active Technical Clubs from Paschimanchal Campus. In this festival, different Project exhibition, Project Competition, Code Camp and Mapathon are held along with real time competitions such as Coding Contest and Circuit designing. This festival provides practical knowledge and competitive spirits to the students of technical field. This festival aims to develop potential of students and also improve the technical sectors all over Nepal. This festival inspires and helps to direct the solutions and ideas developed to be implemented in real life.

VECTOR will have a number of pre-events such as Design Workshop/Contest, Android, Workshop, Python Training, Hardware Training/Contest, Soldering Competition, Seminars, Git Workshop, Django Training and .NET Workshop. These pre-events will promote the event. This will help to improve our event and we will be able to receive more number of visitors and participants. The main events on VECTOR will be project demonstration and competitions on Smart City, Open Hardware, Open Software, Open Mechanical, Civil Modelling, Robo Akhada, Code Camp and Mapathon. We will have other real time competitions such as Code With Coffee Season 2, Circuit Designing Gaming and Fooding Contests.

What is Our Goal?

  • To promote the talents and creativity of students in a wide mass of people from their respective fields.
  • To provide a platform for students to showcase their innovative ideas and projects.
  • To provide a knowledge sharing platform for students so that students from different faculties can have inter-disciplinary knowledge
  • To provide a competitive platform for students and promote the talents and creativity of students and have a reach out at a wide range of peoples from different fields and areas.

Smart City

Smart City

A Smart City is an urban development vision to integrate Information and Communication Technology (ICT) and Internet of Things (IoT) technology in a secure fashion to manage a city's assets. These assets include local department’s information systems, schools, libraries, transportation systems, hospitals, power plants, water supply networks, waste management, law enforcement, and other community services. A smart city is promoted to improve the efficiency of services. Smart City is an emerging new technology that is about to be adopted by different cities in Nepal including Pokhara, Chitwan, Biratnagar, Kathmandu and other major cities. The term ‘Smart City’ itself illustrates the fact that the developmental tasks in a city needs to adopt a smart and efficient technology thus providing effective and reliable services to the citizens with regular monitoring of the services. ICT allows city officials to interact directly with the community and the city infrastructure and to monitor what is happening in the city, how the city is evolving, and how to enable a better quality of life.
